How to Use These Prompts

Before we dive in, a few quick tips to get the most from these ChatGPT prompts for business:

  • Replace the [brackets] with your specific business details. The more specific you are, the better the output.
  • Use any AI tool. These work with ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any major AI assistant.
  • Iterate. Your first response is a starting point. Say "make it more conversational" or "add more detail to point 3" to refine.
  • Save your best prompts. Build a personal library you can reuse every week.
1
Marketing

The 30-Day Content Calendar Generator

Stop staring at a blank screen wondering what to post. This prompt creates an entire month of content in one shot.

I run a [type of business] that serves [target audience]. Our main products/services are [list them]. Create a 30-day social media content calendar for [platform: Instagram/LinkedIn/TikTok]. For each day, include: - Content type (educational, entertaining, promotional, behind-the-scenes) - Post topic and a 1-sentence hook - Suggested hashtags (5-8) - Best time to post Mix content types using an 80/20 rule: 80% value-driven content, 20% promotional. Include 4 posts that tie into trending topics or seasonal events for [current month].
Why it works: Instead of asking for "social media ideas," this prompt gives AI your business context, specifies the output format, and includes a content strategy rule (80/20). You'll get a calendar you can actually use ‚Äî not generic suggestions.
2
Sales

The Cold Email That Actually Gets Replies

Cold outreach is still one of the fastest ways to get clients ‚Äî if your email doesn't sound like every other sales pitch in their inbox.

Write a cold email to [target: e.g., local restaurant owners] offering my [service]. Rules: - First line must reference something specific about their business (I'll fill this in) - Under 120 words total - No corporate jargon or buzzwords - One clear, low-commitment CTA (not "book a call" ‚Äî something easier) - Tone: friendly, confident, not desperate - Include a P.S. line with a relevant stat or quick win Write 3 variations with different angles.
Why it works: The constraints force AI to write like a human, not a marketer. The "3 variations" request gives you options to test. The P.S. line trick increases reply rates by 15-20%.
3
Strategy

The Competitor Analysis Breakdown

Know thy enemy. This prompt turns AI into your competitive intelligence analyst.

Act as a business strategist. I run a [type of business] in [location/market]. My main competitors are [list 2-3 competitors]. For each competitor, analyze: 1. Their apparent target audience 2. Their unique selling proposition 3. Their pricing strategy (based on publicly available info) 4. Their strongest marketing channel 5. One weakness or gap I could exploit Then recommend 3 specific ways I can differentiate my business based on these gaps. Be specific ‚Äî not generic advice like "improve your marketing." Give me actionable moves I can make this week.
Why it works: The "act as" framing puts AI in expert mode. Asking for "actionable moves this week" prevents vague strategic fluff. You get a real competitive playbook.

4
Customer Service

The FAQ and Response Template Builder

Stop answering the same questions over and over. Build a response library once, use it forever.

I run a [type of business]. Here are the 10 most common questions/complaints I get from customers: [List your top 10 questions] For each one, write: 1. A friendly, professional response I can copy-paste (under 100 words) 2. A shorter version for text/DM (under 40 words) 3. If applicable, a way to turn the question into a sales opportunity without being pushy Tone: warm, helpful, and human ‚Äî not robotic corporate speak.
Why it works: Two versions (email and text) cover all your channels. The "sales opportunity" angle is subtle but powerful ‚Äî every FAQ interaction becomes a potential upsell.
5
Marketing

The Email Sequence Writer

Email marketing has the highest ROI of any channel ‚Äî $36 for every $1 spent. This prompt builds a complete nurture sequence.

Write a 5-email welcome sequence for new subscribers to my [type of business] email list. Subscriber context: They signed up for [your lead magnet/freebie] and are interested in [their main pain point]. Email structure: - Email 1 (Day 0): Deliver the freebie + introduce yourself. Warm and personal. - Email 2 (Day 2): Share your best tip related to their pain point. Pure value. - Email 3 (Day 4): Tell a story about a customer transformation / your own journey. - Email 4 (Day 7): Address the #1 objection to buying your [product/service]. - Email 5 (Day 10): Soft pitch for [your offer] with a clear CTA. Each email: subject line + preview text + body under 200 words. Conversational tone ‚Äî write like a friend who happens to be an expert, not a company.
Why it works: The specific day-by-day structure with clear intent for each email follows proven email marketing principles. The 200-word limit keeps emails readable. Most best AI prompts for entrepreneurs lists skip email ‚Äî but this single sequence can generate thousands in revenue.
6
Operations

The Process Documentation Creator

The difference between a freelancer and a business owner: documented processes. This prompt builds your operations manual.

Help me document a standard operating procedure (SOP) for: [process name, e.g., "onboarding a new client"] Here's roughly what I do (in messy notes): [Dump your rough steps, even if they're incomplete or out of order] Turn this into a clean, step-by-step SOP that: 1. Is clear enough for someone with no context to follow 2. Includes estimated time for each step 3. Notes which steps could be automated or delegated 4. Identifies potential failure points and how to prevent them 5. Ends with a quality checklist Format it with headers and numbered steps. Keep the language simple.
Why it works: You can brain-dump messy notes and AI organizes them into a professional SOP. The "automation" and "failure points" additions turn a basic document into a strategic operational tool.
7
Finance

The Pricing Strategy Advisor

Undercharging is the #1 mistake small business owners make. Let AI help you fix that.

Act as a pricing strategist. My business is [describe your business]. I currently charge [current pricing] for [what you deliver]. My costs per client/project are approximately [rough costs]. My competitors charge [competitor pricing range]. Analyze my pricing and: 1. Tell me if I'm undercharging (and by how much, with reasoning) 2. Suggest 3 pricing models I could test (e.g., tiered, value-based, retainer) 3. For each model, show example pricing with projected monthly revenue assuming [number] clients per month 4. Give me a script for communicating a price increase to existing clients Be direct. If my pricing is bad, tell me why.
Why it works: Pricing is emotional for business owners. This prompt forces an objective analysis. The "be direct" instruction prevents AI from sugarcoating. The price increase script is gold ‚Äî most people avoid raising prices because they don't know what to say.
8
Marketing

The Google Business Profile Optimizer

For local businesses, your Google Business Profile is more important than your website. This prompt maximizes it.

I own a [type of local business] in [city/neighborhood]. Help me optimize my Google Business Profile: 1. Write a compelling business description (750 characters max) that naturally includes these keywords: [3-5 local keywords] 2. Suggest 10 Google Posts I should publish over the next month (topic + first sentence for each) 3. Write 5 Q&A entries I should add to my profile (common questions customers search for + optimized answers) 4. Give me a template for responding to positive reviews and a separate template for negative reviews 5. List 5 relevant categories and attributes I should make sure are selected Focus on local SEO. I want to rank in the map pack for [target search term].
Why it works: Most local business AI prompts stop at "write a description." This covers the full GBP optimization ‚Äî posts, Q&A, reviews, categories ‚Äî which is what actually moves rankings.
9
Strategy

The Weekly Business Review Coach

CEOs of big companies do weekly reviews. You should too ‚Äî and AI makes it effortless.

Act as my business coach. Here's my week in review: - Revenue this week: [amount] - New leads/clients: [number] - Biggest win: [describe] - Biggest challenge: [describe] - Hours worked: [number] - Top 3 tasks completed: [list] - Tasks I avoided/procrastinated on: [list] Based on this, give me: 1. An honest assessment of my week (be direct, not encouraging for the sake of it) 2. The ONE thing I should focus on next week to move the needle most 3. One habit or system I should implement based on the pattern you see 4. A specific goal for next week with a measurable outcome
Why it works: Consistent weekly reviews are the habit that separates growing businesses from stagnant ones. AI gives you an objective outside perspective ‚Äî like a business coach without the $500/month price tag.
10
Sales

The Offer Stack Builder

Don't just sell a service ‚Äî sell a transformation. This prompt helps you package your offer so it sells itself.

I sell [your product/service] to [target audience] for [price]. The main result my clients get is: [primary outcome] Help me build an irresistible offer stack: 1. Reframe my offer around the OUTCOME, not the deliverable 2. Suggest 3-5 bonuses I could add that increase perceived value without increasing my costs significantly (digital bonuses, templates, priority access, etc.) 3. Write the offer as a sales page section: headline, 5-7 bullet points of what's included, price anchoring (show the value vs. price), and a CTA 4. Create urgency without being fake (no "only 3 spots left" nonsense ‚Äî give me real, authentic urgency triggers) Make the total perceived value at least 5x the price.
Why it works: This prompt applies proven direct-response copywriting frameworks. The "5x perceived value" instruction and "no fake urgency" rule produce offers that convert without feeling sleazy.

Bonus Tips: Getting Better Results From Every Prompt

These 10 ChatGPT prompts for business will save you hours every week. But to get even better results, keep these principles in mind:

1. Give Context Before Commands

Always tell AI who you are, who you serve, and what you sell before asking it to do something. Context is the #1 factor in output quality.

2. Specify the Format

Want bullet points? Say so. Want a table? Ask for it. Want it under 200 words? Set the limit. AI follows instructions ‚Äî but only if you give them.

3. Use "Act As" for Expert-Level Responses

Starting a prompt with "Act as a [marketing strategist / pricing consultant / copywriter]" dramatically improves the sophistication of the response.

4. Iterate, Don't Start Over

If the first response is 70% right, don't write a new prompt. Say "This is good, but make the tone more casual" or "Expand on point 3." Conversation is AI's superpower.

5. Build a Prompt Library

Save every prompt that works well. Organize them by category. Over time, you'll have a custom toolkit that runs half your business. (Or grab ours ‚Äî see below.)
